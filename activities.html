2025 · Conference
ASPIRE HFES 2025
📍 Chicago, IL

Presented two of our recent works at the HFES 2025 event held in Chicago!

Astronauts experience vestibular impairments induced by altered gravity during spaceflight. My first talk focused on how these vestibular impairments affect postural balance and neural activation using Galvanic Vestibular Stimulation (GVS) and fNIRS neuroimaging.

Coexisting spaceflight hazards such as vestibular impairments and mental fatigue can influence fine motor coordination, which is essential for mission-critical operational tasks. The second talk explained how these interacting factors shape fine motor performance under spaceflight-analog conditions.

🏆 Best Student Work Award — HFES Aerospace Systems Technical Group (ASTG)

2025 · Conference
NASA IWS 2025
📍 Galveston, TX

Fall 2024: Initiated data collection for the first phase of a collaborative project with the Neuroscience Laboratory at NASA Johnson Space Center. Preliminary findings from this work, titled "Characterizing and Mitigating the Interactive Impacts of Fatigue- and Altered Gravity–Related Stressors on Sensorimotor, Behavioral, and Operational Outcomes," were presented at the NASA Human Research Program Investigators' Workshop (IWS) 2025.

2024 · Conference
American Society of Biomechanics — Annual Meeting 2024
📍 Madison, WI

Presented findings from a pilot study conducted to support the setup of our newly funded NASA project investigating the interactive effects of altered gravity and fatigue. The study involved 13 participants and helped identify potential challenges and necessary refinements to the experimental setup prior to the launch of the full-scale experiment, anticipated to begin in Fall 2024.
