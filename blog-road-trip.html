A Summer Road Trip with Friends Through the Great Lakes Region

2024 · 5 min read

Last summer, we set out on a road trip with our friends, chasing lakes, forests, cliffs, and small coastal towns across the Upper Midwest. It turned into one of those trips where every stop felt different, but all of them shared the same peaceful, natural beauty.

We explored parts of Michigan and Minnesota, covering long scenic drives, quiet hikes, and unforgettable lake views.

Our Stops

01
🏜️ Michigan
Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore
Our journey began where massive sand dunes meet the deep blue waters of Lake Michigan. Climbing the dunes was both fun and exhausting — every step forward felt like sliding half a step back. But when we finally reached the top, the view made everything worth it. Endless shoreline, soft wind, and a calm horizon made it feel almost unreal. We stayed longer than planned, just sitting on the sand.
02
🌊 Upper Peninsula, Michigan
Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore
One of the most beautiful places on the trip — colorful cliffs rising above Lake Superior. Shades of red, orange, and green blended into the rock walls, shaped by wind and water over time. We took a boat tour along the shoreline, which gave us a completely different view. From the water, everything looked even more dramatic and powerful.
03
🌲 Upper Peninsula, Michigan
Driving the Upper Peninsula
Driving through the Upper Peninsula itself was an experience on its own. Long roads, dense forests, and very few crowds made it feel peaceful and remote. We stopped at small roadside viewpoints, ate simple snacks in the car, and enjoyed the feeling of being far away from busy city life. The quietness of the region was something we all appreciated deeply.
04
💦 Minnesota
Tettegouche State Park
Crossing into Minnesota, we found a place filled with waterfalls, rocky trails, and views of Lake Superior. We hiked to waterfalls surrounded by tall trees and rocky cliffs. The sound of rushing water mixed with the wind created a calming atmosphere. A great place to slow down after long hours of driving.
05
🐟 Leland, Michigan
Fishtown in Leland
On our way back, we stopped at this small historic fishing village that felt like stepping into another time. Old shanties, wooden docks, and small shops made the area very charming. We walked around, watched fishing boats, and enjoyed fresh local snacks by the water. Simple but one of the most memorable stops because of its peaceful and authentic atmosphere.

🚗 Final Thoughts

This road trip wasn't just about the destinations — it was about the journey between them. Long drives, shared laughter with friends, spontaneous stops, and quiet moments by the lakes all made it special.

The Great Lakes region has a way of slowing everything down. Every place we visited felt natural, calm, and refreshing. If you ever get the chance to road trip through this part of the country, don't overthink it — just go.
