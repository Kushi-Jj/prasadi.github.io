Research

What I Work On

My research sits at the intersection of neuroergonomics, human factors, and engineering — with the goal of designing systems that work with the human body, not against it.

Current Project Active

Astronaut Sensorimotor Performance Under Altered Gravity & Fatigue

Working with NASA-relevant scenarios to model how fatigue and microgravity compound to degrade human sensorimotor control. The goal is to inform adaptive assistance systems for long-duration spaceflight — combining wearable sensing, deep learning-based classification, and biomechanical modeling to build a real-time performance monitoring pipeline.

Focus Areas

Research Themes

🧠

Neuroergonomics

Understanding neural and cognitive mechanisms underlying human performance in real-world operational environments, especially under stress and fatigue.

🤖

Human–Machine Interaction

Designing assistive devices and exoskeleton systems with mechatronic integration, biosignal-driven control, and adaptive human-in-the-loop architectures.

🛸

Space Human Factors

Investigating how altered gravity environments challenge human physiology, cognition, and motor performance during extended missions.

📡

Wearable Sensing

EMG, EEG, and IMU-based systems for real-time physiological monitoring, fatigue classification, and intent recognition.

🧬

Biomechanics

Musculoskeletal analysis and motion capture for understanding human movement, postural control, and injury risk assessment.

🔬

Deep Learning & Signal Processing

Temporal models (LSTM, Transformers) and biosignal processing pipelines for classifying human performance states from multi-modal data.
